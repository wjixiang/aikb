## 3. Key Themes and Findings

### 3.1 Surgical Innovations

**Unilateral Biportal Endoscopic (UBE) Discectomy** emerges as a major focus in recent literature:
- Multiple studies compare UBE versus microdiscectomy with comparable clinical outcomes
- Novel K-point approach preserves bone and ligamentum flavum structures
- Risk factors for reoperation and residual pain post-UBE have been identified
- Far lateral disc herniations treated effectively with either transforaminal or biportal endoscopy

**Percutaneous Endoscopic Lumbar Discectomy (PELD)**:
- Compared favorably to microdiscectomy with similar analgesic requirements
- Ultrasound-guided erector spinae plane block improves pain management during PELD

**Revision Surgery**:
- Systematic review addresses risk factors and timing for revision discectomy versus fusion in recurrent herniation

### 3.2 Conservative Management

**Physical Therapy Approaches**:
- Combined aquatic and Pilates exercises significantly improve pain, mobility, functional disability, and quality of life in women with lumbar disc herniation
- Manual therapy combined with functional magnetic stimulation shows short-term benefits for radiculopathy

**Traditional Chinese Medicine**:
- Finite element analysis demonstrates biomechanical effects of Tuina treatment for lumbar disc herniation

**Natural Resorption**:
- Systematic review identifies predictive factors for spontaneous resorption of herniated discs

### 3.3 Special Considerations

**Pediatric Population**:
- COVID-19 pandemic lockdown associated with changes in pediatric lumbosacral disc herniation presentation and outcomes

**Psychological Factors**:
- Kinesiophobia (fear of movement) significantly associated with disability, poor quality of life, and surgery dissatisfaction post-microdiscectomy

**Cervical Disc Herniation**:
- Case report documents complete relief with conservative treatment in a young patient with massive cervical herniation

### 3.4 Basic Science

**Nucleus Pulposus Biology**:
- Review article explores TIE2-positive cells in the nucleus pulposus and their role in disc herniation pathophysiology

## 4. Treatment Algorithm Summary

Based on the literature, treatment approaches can be categorized as follows:

### First Line (Conservative)
- Physical therapy (aquatic exercises, Pilates)
- Manual therapy with functional magnetic stimulation
- Tuina and other traditional techniques
- Watchful waiting with natural resorption potential

### Second Line (Interventional)
- Percutaneous endoscopic lumbar discectomy (PELD)
- Microdiscectomy
- Unilateral biportal endoscopic (UBE) discectomy

### Third Line (Surgical)
- Revision discectomy for recurrent herniation
- Fusion procedures for select cases

## 5. Research Gaps and Future Directions

1. **Long-term outcomes** of endoscopic techniques compared to traditional microdiscectomy
2. **Predictive models** for natural resorption to guide conservative vs. surgical decision-making
3. **Rehabilitation protocols** optimizing functional recovery across different surgical approaches
4. **Psychological interventions** addressing kinesiophobia to improve surgical outcomes
5. **Biological therapies** targeting nucleus pulposus regeneration

---

## 6. Conclusion

The 2026 literature on intervertebral disc herniation treatment reflects a shift toward minimally invasive endoscopic techniques (particularly unilateral biportal endoscopy), while maintaining evidence for the efficacy of conservative management including targeted physical therapy. The field continues to evolve with improved surgical technologies, better understanding of natural disease course (resorption), and recognition of psychological factors affecting patient outcomes.
